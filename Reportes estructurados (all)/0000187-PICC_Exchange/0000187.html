PICC Exchange

After the procedure was explained to the patient, the patient was brought to the interventional radiology suite and placed supine on the exam table. The upper extremity and current PICC line were prepped and draped in usual sterile fashion. 1% lidocaine was used for local anesthetic.

Findings

The PICC line was pulled back approximately 20 cm and cut at a 90-degree angle. Through the remaining portion of the PICC line, a Microvena wire was advanced centrally into the IVC. The remaining portion of the PICC line was removed.

A 5 French vascular peel-away sheath was then placed over the wire into the vein.

The new PICC line was sized to cm. The inner dilator and wire were removed. Through the sheath, a PICC line was placed such that the tip was at the junction of SVC and right atrium. This was confirmed with fluoroscopy.

The catheter was secured in place with 3-0 nonabsorbable suture. The catheter aspirates and flushes without difficulty. A sterile dressing was applied.
